This is the single source of truth for IronLink's confirmation system — every modal decision the app ever asks, laid out side by side rather than inside one phone frame. That gallery layout is intentional: it lets an auditor verify the whole dialog family at a glance (icon variant, title voice, button order, destructive treatment) instead of hunting through 17 separate screens. The key UX decision is consistency of destructive affordance: every irreversible action uses the --danger icon tint plus a btn--danger control, with the safe/cancel option always reachable and never styled as the primary; positive and neutral confirms use --pos/--info/--warn tints with a yellow primary (worker-positive) or dark button (employer/neutral). Two-button confirms sit in a row; three-tier or "stacked" choices (Publish, Cancel subscription, Delete account, Payment failed) stack vertically with the recommended action on top so a lower-literacy user reads top-down. Info dialogs (OTP expired, Payment failed) are non-blocking recoveries with a forward action, not a yes/no. The two action sheets handle multi-option moderation (Report) and a safety confirm (Block) from a bottom sheet.
